From Foundations to certification standard

Clinical Skills follows on from Imago Foundations and takes you through the full range of Imago processes to certification standard. You book both five-day blocks together as one commitment, and by the end of the ten days you will have completed all 12 taught training days required before entering the certification process.

Prerequisite: you must have completed the Imago Foundations course before attending Clinical Skills.

A modality practised worldwide, taught here through daily practice

Imago Relationship Therapy has been practised internationally for decades, with a defined curriculum, international oversight, and a recognised certification process. What distinguishes this training is not only the modality itself, but the way it is taught: through repeated, supervised, supported practice.

Theory alone does not prepare you for high conflict, shutdown, ruptures in trust, or the moments when you need to know what to do next in the room. Daily practice helps the method become something you can actually use with couples, rather than something you are still trying to work out live.

What you will learn

Clinical Skills is delivered in two five-day blocks. The first deepens the foundations; the second takes you into more advanced processes, difficult presentations, and live supervision.

Block 1

Days 3 to 7

Deepening the method

  • Deepening and extending the Imago Dialogue Process.
  • Holding a couple in dialogue through resistance and difficulty.
  • Working with the Couples Dialogue across a full session structure.
  • Understanding relationship patterns through Imago theory.
  • Introducing the Behaviour Change Request process.
  • Daily practice in triads with peer and trainer feedback.

Block 2

Days 8 to 12

Working with complexity

  • Advanced Imago processes and interventions.
  • Working with high-conflict couples and entrenched patterns.
  • Supporting couples through affairs and ruptures in trust.
  • Working with anger safely and effectively in the therapy room.
  • Live supervision of your own couple cases.
  • Consolidating your clinical identity as an Imago Relationship Therapist.

Outcome

By the end

Where this training takes you

By the end of all ten days, you will have worked through the full range of core Imago processes many times, with feedback, repetition, and supervision built in.

That repetition is what helps the approach feel grounded and usable in real sessions with your own couples.

Face-to-face or online

Clinical Skills runs in two formats: face-to-face at the Affinity Centre in Cheadle, and online via Zoom. Both cover the same content with the same trainers.

Face-to-face

Attend in person at the Affinity Centre in Cheadle and complete the full ten days with your cohort in the room.

Online via Zoom

Join live online and complete the full ten days with the same cohort and trainers in a real-time interactive format.

How format choice works

  • You choose one format when you book and stay with that cohort for all ten days.
  • You cannot move between formats once Clinical Skills begins.
  • You can attend Foundations in either format and choose Clinical Skills format separately.

A training that reflects the diversity of your clients

The couples you work with will come from different backgrounds, cultures, identities, and relationship structures. Some will be neurodivergent, and some will be navigating forms of complexity that standard training texts do not address well.

Clinical Skills works with that reality rather than around it. The training aims to help you notice and honour the complexity each couple brings, while also making space for therapists from different backgrounds to learn as their whole selves.

We actively welcome

  • Neurodivergent therapists.
  • Therapists from diverse racial and cultural backgrounds.
  • Therapists of all sexualities, gender identities, and relationship perspectives.

What you need to attend

You must have completed the Imago Foundations course before booking Clinical Skills.

You do not need to already be seeing couples in your practice, although some participants will be. The key requirement is that you have completed the first two days and are ready to continue into the advanced stage of the pathway.

What comes next

Once you have completed all 12 training days, you are ready to begin the certification process.

Step 1

Complete training

All 12 days

Foundations plus Clinical Skills completes the taught part of the Imago training pathway.

Step 2

Supervision and assessment

After training

  • Six months of supervision on your couple casework.
  • A final video submission.

Step 3

Workshop requirement

At any point during training

Certification also requires attendance at a Getting the Love You Want couples workshop or a Finding and Keeping Love singles workshop. Taking part earlier gives you a grounding in the approach from the client’s perspective.

Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about prerequisites, format, booking, certification, and payment.

What is Imago Clinical Skills training?

It is the advanced stage of Imago Relationship Therapy training, covering Days 3 to 12 of the full pathway. Delivered in two blocks of five days, it develops your practice through the full range of Imago clinical processes, including live supervision of your own couple cases.

Do I need to have done Imago Foundations first?

Yes. Completion of the Imago Foundations course is a prerequisite for attending Clinical Skills.

Do I have to book both blocks at the same time?

Yes. Clinical Skills is booked as a single commitment covering both five-day blocks.

Is the training available online?

Yes. Clinical Skills is offered face-to-face in Cheadle and online via Zoom, with the same content and trainers in both formats.

Can I mix face-to-face and online across the two blocks?

No. You choose one format when you book and complete all ten days with that cohort. Foundations can be taken in a different format.

What level of couples therapy experience do I need?

You need to have completed Imago Foundations. You do not need to already be seeing couples in your practice, although some participants are.

What is involved in the certification process after the training?

After completing all 12 days, certification requires six months of supervised couple casework, a video submission, and attendance at a Getting the Love You Want or Finding and Keeping Love workshop.

Are payment plans available?

Yes. If cost is a barrier, instalment plans are available.

Can these hours count towards CTA qualification?

Yes. Joanna holds PTSTA status, so hours completed on this training can count towards CTA qualification.
