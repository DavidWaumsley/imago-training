Frequently asked questions

Quick answers to the most common questions about the pathway, formats, and certification.

What is Imago Relationship Therapy?

Imago Relationship Therapy is a structured therapeutic approach developed specifically for working with couples. Created by Harville Hendrix and Helen LaKelly Hunt in 1980, it combines relational theory with a practical dialogue process that therapists can learn and apply directly in sessions. It is practised by thousands of therapists in nearly 50 countries.

Who is this training for?

It is designed for qualified or training counsellors and psychotherapists who want to specialise in couples work. You do not need prior couples experience to begin.

How does the modular pathway work?

You can start with the two-day Foundations course, then decide whether to continue into the ten-day Clinical Skills stage. After all 12 days, certification is completed through supervision and a video submission.

Do I have to commit to all 12 days at once?

No. You can begin with Foundations first. Clinical Skills is booked as one commitment, but that decision comes after the introductory stage.

Is the online version different from the in-person course?

The online version is live and interactive on Zoom. It is not a recording or self-study course, and it follows the same training pathway.

Can I mix online and in-person formats?

You can choose either format for Foundations. For Clinical Skills, you stay with one cohort for the full ten days.

How do I become certified?

Certification follows successful completion of all 12 training days, at least six months of supervision, and one video submission.

Can these hours count towards CTA training?

Yes. Because Joanna Groves holds PTSTA status, training hours can count towards CTA qualification.

Is the training inclusive?

The training is designed to welcome different identities, backgrounds, and learning needs, and intersectionality is embedded throughout the course content.
